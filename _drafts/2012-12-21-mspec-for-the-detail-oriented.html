I'm a slave to detail in my coding life. I like coding conventions and I follow them religiously. When you're on a team of developers, coding conventions go beyond indentations and capitalization. There are dozens of decisions we make every day when we write code. When I first joined my team, MSpec was in its infancy on our project. We had very few tests and only a few loosely held conventions. 

I saw a blank slate. 

There are a few commonly held beliefs about how specs should be laid out. Beyond this, you're free to wing it.
